Anna Lundquist

My name is Anna and I'm 23 years old from Stockholm, Sweden. I play professional basketball for Norrk√∂ping Dolphins in the Swedish league and study Psychology at the University.

I was born into a basketball family. My two sisters and I started playing basketball at age 4 with my parents as coaches. I have one older and one younger sister. Every summer, we played basketball and many other games at our summer house. My whole family is competitive ( and bad losers) so our summers consisted of a series of intense competitions. We also ran through forest together to get conditioning workouts. We enjoyed pushing each other to the limit. When Elsa joined my basketball team I gained another great workout partner. We spent a lot of time in the gym working our asses off. We have always had our mind on our business and always do one more rep to push ourselves through exhaustion. Together with Elsa I've built my toughness and believe in what I can achieve if I only i do more than I expect from myself.

After highschool I left for college in the United States. I played in NCAA division 1 for Siena Saints, and studied Environmental Studies in New York State. I grew a lot as a person during the 2 years of my stay. The biggest lesson i probably learnt was that in order to be successful, I have to give everything I have, and expect nothing in return. I did this with patience and believe in myself. College taught me discipline and I learnt to adjust to a different system, with different rules from what I was used to.

I have also been lucky achieve one of my big goals with basketball - to play for the national team of Sweden in multiple tournaments. I have played in and against various countries.

With experiences of playing all over the world, I have gained a deep understanding in different cultures and learned a lot about other people. Thanks to basketball, I've opened my eyes to the world.

I have a deep interest in global sustainability. I believe that since we only have one planet, we have to take care of it. And as important, we have to take care of each other on this one planet. Imagine a world where we take each others hands and help each other stand strong, then we can bounce together.

I have been extremely lucky with the resources in my career. I've had courts to practice and play on, basketball shoes and clothes to play in, and coaches to help me develop my game. I have spent hours sweating and fighting to achieve my goals. I have achieved a lot of my goals, and as I have achieved them, I have found new goals. Today, my greatest goal is to be able to create opportunities for others to achieve their dreams. With My experince I want to give the right mindset and tools to people who have ambitions and but need support and a push in the right direction towards their goals. This is why I do this project - I want to give back what I have been opportuned to have.

This project means a lot to me. Basketball is not just a sport for me, it is a lifestyle and a tool to approach dreams. It is an opportunity to be a part of something bigger than yourself. Basketball creates excitement, dreams, friends, family, respect, and joy. Basketball brings people together. I see a bright future with basketball, and I want to use it to make the world a better place.
